Hi, I'm Nathan Stitt, the creator of the IFR Approach plate bundler.

I did this becouse I was sick of attempting to fold the huge NACO chart booklet just right so I could see the approach, then attempting to clip it to my kneeboard. Invariably, it'd slip out just about the time I reached the FAF and realized I was thinking 'what were the minimum's again?' I'm sure that that never happens to anyone else...

The Jeppesen charts are the way to go with their booklet format, but I felt the eastern US subscription was too expensive for the amount of use I'd get out of them.

For awhile I simply printed each one that I thought I'd need, but that was unwieldly as well. Picture a huge stack of 8 1/2 x 11 paper flying around the cockpit and you get an idea of the mess.

I then started downloading the charts from NACO, opening each one in Inkscape, rotating and scaling them to make a booklet and printing them. That worked great, except it took forever.

I then decided Hey, I'm a programmer, lets fix this, how hard can it be? Of course no sooner than I finished it I becae aware of other solutions such as:

  • NACOmatic - Makes custom PDFs of entires states available to download.
Of course, I still like my solution the best. Some of the technical details are:

  • The code behind the site is a custom apache module written in C/C++. By day I'm a ruby/rails programmer. Don't get me wrong, I love rails, but was looking for something to take me back to the good 'ole C/C++ days. Therefore, I chose to write this in a fasion I never would have done for a real project. Surprisingly it worked out very well, and didn't seem to take much longer to write than a comparably Rails project.
  • I can wholehartedly recommend CMake over autoconf / automake. I hope to never write a configure.ac file again.
  • For the JS, I chose JQuery, simply becouse I was getting rusty with it after spending quite a bit of time with Prototype lately. I was also pleased with JQuery, but I did miss the nice JS additions prototype offers. Of course this site isn't really cutting edge or complex.
  • If you are really interested, I've open-sourced the code and thrown it up on github.
